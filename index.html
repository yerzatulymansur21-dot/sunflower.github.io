Overview

SUNFLOWER is a sensorless solar tracking concept: instead of optical sensors, it uses location + date + time to compute the Sun’s position and control panel orientation. The aim is to improve real-world reliability by reducing sensitivity to dust, clouds, and shading.

Where the idea came from

Conventional trackers often rely on light sensors. In real conditions, sensors can lose the Sun, triggering unnecessary searching movements, energy waste, and maintenance. SUNFLOWER avoids this by using solar position mathematics (NOAA/SPA-style).

Main advantages

  • No optical sensors → less sensitivity to dust & shading
  • Fewer unnecessary movements → lower wear and energy use
  • Stable control logic from astronomy-based calculations

Why it matters: Climate Change

Global warming is one of the main challenges of the 21st century. The burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) increases greenhouse gas emissions and contributes to rising global temperature. Improving renewable energy efficiency helps reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

Formulas (Solar Position Math)

Key equations used to compute solar elevation and azimuth (NOAA/SPA-style).

Fractional year

\[ \gamma_f = \frac{2\pi}{365}\left(d - 1 + \frac{x - 12}{24}\right) \]

Equation of Time

\[ E_t = 229.18\left( 0.000075 + 0.001868\cos\gamma_f - 0.032077\sin\gamma_f - 0.014615\cos(2\gamma_f) - 0.040849\sin(2\gamma_f) \right) \]

Solar declination

\[ \delta = 0.006918 - 0.399912\cos\gamma_f + 0.070257\sin\gamma_f - 0.006758\cos(2\gamma_f) + 0.000907\sin(2\gamma_f) - 0.002697\cos(3\gamma_f) + 0.00148\sin(3\gamma_f) \]

Solar elevation

\[ \sin(\alpha) = \sin(\varphi)\sin(\delta) + \cos(\varphi)\cos(\delta)\cos(H) \]

Solar azimuth

\[ \gamma = \operatorname{atan2}\left(\sin(H),\, \cos(H)\sin(\varphi)-\tan(\delta)\cos(\varphi)\right) \]

Physics & Energy

Annual energy estimate

\[ E = H \cdot PR \]

where \(E\) is annual energy (kWh/kWp·year), \(H\) is annual irradiation on the plane of array, and \(PR\) is performance ratio (typical 0.75–0.85).

Tracking gain

\[ E_{\text{tracker}} = E_{\text{fixed}}(1+g) \]

Example gain

\[ \Delta E = 1700 - 1350 = 350\ \text{kWh/kWp·year} \] \[ g = \frac{350}{1350} \approx 0.26 = 26\% \]
