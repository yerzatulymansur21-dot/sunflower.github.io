1) Overview

SUNFLOWER is a sensorless solar tracking control concept that replaces optical sensors with algorithm-based control. Instead of relying on light intensity, SUNFLOWER computes the Sun’s position from location + date + time and outputs motor commands for panel orientation.

  • No optical sensors → less sensitivity to dust, clouds, shading.
  • Reduced maintenance and fewer unnecessary corrective movements.
  • Comparable tracking gains with improved stability in real-world conditions.

2) Solar Position Mathematics

SUNFLOWER uses standard astronomical solar position equations (NOAA/SPA-style approach). The goal is to compute solar elevation \(\alpha\) and solar azimuth \(\gamma\).

2.1 Variables

  • \(\varphi\) — latitude
  • \(\delta\) — solar declination
  • \(H\) — hour angle
  • \(\alpha\) — solar elevation angle
  • \(\gamma\) — solar azimuth angle
  • \(\gamma_f\) — fractional year (NOAA)
  • \(E_t\) — equation of time (minutes)

2.2 Fractional year (NOAA)

Fractional year angle represents Earth’s position along its orbit based on day number and local time.

\[ \gamma_f = \frac{2\pi}{365}\left(d - 1 + \frac{x - 12}{24}\right) \]

where \(d\) is the day of year and \(x\) is local time in hours (decimal).

2.3 Equation of Time (NOAA)

The equation of time corrects the difference between mean solar time and true solar time.

\[ E_t = 229.18\left( 0.000075 + 0.001868\cos\gamma_f - 0.032077\sin\gamma_f - 0.014615\cos(2\gamma_f) - 0.040849\sin(2\gamma_f) \right) \]

2.4 Solar declination (NOAA)

Solar declination describes how far the Sun is from the equator line (roughly \(-23.44^\circ\) to \(+23.44^\circ\)).

\[ \delta = 0.006918 - 0.399912\cos\gamma_f + 0.070257\sin\gamma_f - 0.006758\cos(2\gamma_f) + 0.000907\sin(2\gamma_f) - 0.002697\cos(3\gamma_f) + 0.00148\sin(3\gamma_f) \]

2.5 Solar Elevation

\[ \sin(\alpha) = \sin(\varphi)\sin(\delta) + \cos(\varphi)\cos(\delta)\cos(H) \]

2.6 Solar Azimuth (one common form)

\[ \gamma = \operatorname{atan2}\left(\sin(H),\, \cos(H)\sin(\varphi)-\tan(\delta)\cos(\varphi)\right) \]

Note: In a full implementation, solar time correction (Equation of Time) and time-zone conversion are included. This page provides the key core equations used for Sun positioning.

3) Physics & Energy Production

3.1 Annual energy estimate

A common PV engineering estimate for annual energy per installed capacity is:

\[ E = H \cdot PR \]

where \(E\) is annual energy (kWh/kWp·year), \(H\) is annual irradiation on the plane of array (kWh/kWp·year), and \(PR\) is the performance ratio (typical 0.75–0.85).

3.2 Tracking gain comparison

If tracking improves yield by \(g\), then:

\[ E_{\text{tracker}} = E_{\text{fixed}}(1+g) \]

3.3 Example values (benchmark)

Fixed: \(E_{\text{fixed}} \approx 1350\ \text{kWh/kWp·year}\)
SUNFLOWER: \(E_{\text{SUNFLOWER}} \approx 1700\ \text{kWh/kWp·year}\)

\[ \Delta E = 1700 - 1350 = 350\ \text{kWh/kWp·year} \] \[ g = \frac{350}{1350} \approx 0.26 = 26\% \]

3.4 Convert energy gain to revenue

With electricity price \(p = 0.10\ \text{USD/kWh}\):

\[ \Delta R = \Delta E \cdot p = 350 \cdot 0.10 = 35\ \text{USD/kWp·year} \]

These are benchmark numbers for demonstration. You can later replace them with PVGIS or PVWatts results for your specific location.
